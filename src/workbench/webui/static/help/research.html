Deep Research

Overview

Deep Research performs tree-structured investigation of any question. It recursively explores sub-questions, synthesizes findings, and produces a comprehensive markdown report. Progress streams in real-time via SSE.

Research Question

Enter a clear, well-scoped research question. Examples: "What are the environmental impacts of lithium mining?", "Compare Rust and Go for systems programming", "How do transformer neural networks work?"

Report Title

Optional. A custom title for the generated report. If left blank, the research question is used as the title.

Tree Depth

Controls how deep the research tree goes (1–5). Higher values produce more thorough research but take exponentially longer. Each level generates sub-questions from the previous level's findings. Default: 3.

Branching Factor

How many sub-questions to generate per node (1–10). Each branch becomes a parallel research task. Higher values increase breadth and total compute. Default: 3.

Warning: Max depth 5 + max branching 10 = up to 10^5 = 100,000 leaf nodes. Use conservative values for initial exploration.

Report Language

Select the output language for the final report. The research process and sub-question generation adapt to the chosen language.

Start Research

Click to begin. The button shows loading state. Research progress appears as a live tree visualization with per-node status indicators. The final report renders as formatted markdown.

Stop

Abort a running research session. The results collected so far are preserved.

Export & Copy

  • Copy Markdown — copies the full report to clipboard
  • Export PDF — generates a PDF with your chosen template
  • New Research — clears the results and returns to the input form

Template Selector

Choose a PDF template for export. Templates control styling, fonts, and layout.

Past Research

Expand the past sessions section to view previous research runs. Click a session to load its report.

Tip: For broad topics, start with depth 2 and branching 3. Increase only after reviewing initial results.
