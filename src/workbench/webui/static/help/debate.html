Debate Arena

Overview

The Debate Arena orchestrates multi-agent debates on any topic. Each agent assumes a role (optimist, pessimist, pragmatist, etc.) and argues from that perspective. The arena runs multiple rounds with cross-examination.

Topic

Enter the debate topic or question. Be specific — the quality of the debate depends on a well-formed prompt. Example: "Should artificial general intelligence be developed with open-source models?"

Max Rounds

Set the number of debate rounds (1–10). Each round consists of opening statements, rebuttals, and cross-examination. More rounds produce deeper analysis but take longer. Default: 3 rounds.

Panel Roles

Select 2–8 roles for the debate panel. Each role has a distinct perspective and argumentation style:

  • Optimist — argues for positive outcomes and opportunities
  • Pessimist — identifies risks, downsides, and failure modes
  • Pragmatist — focuses on practical implementation and trade-offs
  • Additional roles may be available depending on configuration

Start Debate

Click to begin the debate. The button shows a loading state while agents are initialized. The debate runs asynchronously — the UI polls for updates every few seconds.

Debate Controls

  • Pause — temporarily halt the debate mid-round
  • Resume — continue a paused debate
  • End Debate — terminate the debate early (combines to "Back to Setup" on completion)

Director Mode

Inject your own content into an active debate. Enter text in the "Content" field and set a weight (1–10) to control how strongly the injected content influences the debate. Click "Inject" to insert it into the next round.

Past Debates

Expand the past sessions section to view previous debates. Each entry shows the topic, date, and status. Click to load the full debate transcript.

Tip: Use Director Mode to steer debates toward specific angles or to introduce facts the agents might overlook.
